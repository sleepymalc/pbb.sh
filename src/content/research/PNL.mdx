**TL;DR.** **Pseudo-Nonlinear (PNL) augmentation** embeds data into a *dually-flat statistical manifold* $\mathcal{S}$, encodes it by KL-projection onto a designed $e$-flat sub-manifold $\mathcal{B}$, and decodes by a *backward projection* onto a local data sub-manifold $\mathcal{D}$ built from $k$ nearest neighbors — turning data augmentation into two closed-form information-geometric projections, with the order $\ell$ of mode interactions retained as the only knob.

Learning-based augmentation is most valuable when data is scarce, yet that is exactly when a high-quality generative model is hardest to train; learning-free baselines (PCA, SVD) are interpretable but reconstruct poorly. PNL sidesteps both by *designing* the latent space rather than learning it: the geometry of $\mathcal{S}$ is fixed by the energy parameters of a log-linear model on posets, and the practitioner specifies which statistical interactions are preserved by choosing a sub-manifold $\mathcal{M}_\ell$. Both projections are convex and admit efficient solvers.

## Why PNL

- **Learning-free.** The latent manifold $\mathcal{B}$ is constructed analytically from the poset structure and the choice of $\ell$ — no generative model is trained, no decoder is fit. Both projections reduce to convex programs in $O(\lvert B\rvert)$ gradient cost.
- **Interpretable & controllable.** Choosing $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{M}_\ell$ explicitly fixes the order of mode interactions retained: small $\ell$ preserves coarse shape, larger $\ell$ preserves fine color-texture coupling.
- **Pseudo-nonlinear by design.** Perturbations that are *linear* in the manifold's intrinsic $\theta$-coordinates become *curved* in the ambient data space — recovering nonlinear augmentations from a linear interpolation in $\mathcal{B}$.
- **Stability where it matters.** On Connectionist Bench (208 samples), PNL cuts the test-accuracy standard deviation from **8.58% → 4.54%** and lifts mean accuracy **88.10% → 93.81%** — the regime where learning-based augmentation breaks down.
- **Modality-agnostic.** A single framework covers images (MNIST, CIFAR-10), audio (Speech Commands), and tabular data (Connectionist Bench, Taiwanese Bankruptcy, Wine Quality).

## Method

### From data to manifold: the normalizing embedding

We treat each data point as a *real-valued poset*. Given a finite poset $(\Omega, \leq)$ with bottom element $\bot$, a $D$-dimensional vector, matrix, or tensor naturally maps to a function $z\colon \Omega \to \mathbb{R}$ — for a positive tensor $P \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}^{I_1 \times \cdots \times I_d}$ with index set $\Omega = [I_1] \times \cdots \times [I_d]$, the natural partial order is coordinate-wise: $v \leq w \iff i_k \leq j_k$ for all $k$.

The *normalizing embedding* $\varphi \colon \Omega_{\mathbb{R}} \to \mathcal{S}$ turns each $z$ into a discrete probability distribution on $\Omega$. For positive tensors,

$$
\bigl(\varphi(P)\bigr)_v \;\coloneqq\; \frac{P_v}{\sum_{w \in \Omega} P_w} \qquad \forall\, v \in \Omega,
$$

so $\varphi(z) \in \mathcal{S}$ where $\mathcal{S} = \{p \colon \Omega \to \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \mid \sum_x p(x) = 1\}$ is the $(\lvert\Omega\rvert-1)$-dimensional manifold of discrete distributions on $\Omega$. The *log-linear model on posets* equips $\mathcal{S}$ with dually-flat coordinates $(\theta, \eta)$ via $\log p(x) = \sum_{y \leq x} \theta(y)$: $\theta$ is the natural parameter (energy), $\eta$ is the expectation parameter, and the Legendre duality $\psi \leftrightarrow \psi^*$ gives Bregman divergences whose dual is the KL divergence $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p, q) = \sum_x p(x)\log\bigl(p(x)/q(x)\bigr)$.

For positive tensors, the natural poset is the product of coordinate-wise orders, illustrated alongside for a $3$-mode tensor — each interior node is a tuple $(i_1, i_2, i_3)$ whose ancestors are the entries with all coordinates $\leq$ this one. This is the substrate on which $\ell$-body sub-manifolds are defined.

### Encoder: KL projection onto $\mathcal{B}$

To compress, we pick an $e$-flat sub-manifold $\mathcal{B} \subseteq \mathcal{S}$ with $\dim(\mathcal{B}) \ll \dim(\mathcal{S})$ and *project* $\varphi(z)$ onto it via the $m$-projection — the unique minimizer of KL divergence:

$$
\operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{B}}(p) \;=\; \arg\min_{q \in \mathcal{B}}\, D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p, q) \;=\; \arg\min_{q \in \mathcal{B}} \sum_{x \in \Omega} p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}.
$$

Because $\mathcal{B}$ is $e$-flat, this is a *convex* program in the $\theta$-coordinates; the gradient $\partial D_{\mathrm{KL}}/\partial \theta = \eta - \eta^{p}$ has a closed form, so natural-gradient or vanilla gradient descent converges in polynomial time. The encoder is then

$$
\mathsf{Enc} \;\coloneqq\; \operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{B}} \circ \varphi \colon \Omega_{\mathbb{R}} \to \mathcal{B}.
$$

Uniqueness is guaranteed by Amari's projection theorem: flatness + KL convexity ⇒ a unique projection destination.

### $\ell$-body approximation as a design knob

The choice of $\mathcal{B}$ is where modeling enters. For positive tensors, the *many-body approximation* gives a principled hierarchy. An *$\ell$-body parameter* is a $\theta(x)$ whose index $x \in \Omega$ has exactly $\ell$ non-one coordinates — it encodes the interaction among $\ell$ different modes. The $\ell$-body sub-manifold zeroes out higher-order interactions:

$$
\mathcal{M}_\ell \;\coloneqq\; \bigl\{ \theta \in \mathbb{R}^{\dim(\mathcal{S})} \,\bigm|\, \theta_x = 0 \text{ for all non-}\ell\text{-body parameters } x \in \Omega \bigr\}.
$$

Setting $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{M}_\ell$ retains interactions of order $\leq \ell$ and *discards* every higher-order coupling — small $\ell$ keeps coarse structure (shape), larger $\ell$ keeps fine joint statistics (shape × color). Because the constraint is linear in $\theta$, $\mathcal{M}_\ell$ is $e$-flat, so the encoder remains a convex KL minimization. There is an intrinsic trade-off: $\dim(\mathcal{B}) \to \dim(\mathcal{S})$ preserves more information but invites the curse of dimensionality at the augmenting step; $\ell$ is the lever.

### Decoder: backward projection

Decoding an augmented latent $w^* \in \mathcal{B}$ back to data space is harder — $\mathsf{Enc}$ is not invertible. Rather than fit a separate decoder, PNL exploits the *data itself*: assume similar points project to similar latents, so the inverse of $w^*$ should live near the pre-images of its latent neighbors.

Formally, given the embedded dataset $\{z_i' = \varphi(z_i)\}_{i=1}^n$ with encodings $\{w_i = \operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{B}}(z_i')\}_{i=1}^n$, find $w^*$'s $k$-nearest neighbors $N \subseteq [n]$ in $\mathcal{B}$ and build the *local data sub-manifold* — the dual of $\mathcal{M}_\ell$, fixing the $\ell$-body coordinates to the neighborhood mean while leaving everything else free:

$$
\mathcal{M}_\ell^{\ast}(N) \;\coloneqq\; \left\{ \theta \in \mathbb{R}^{\dim(\mathcal{S})} \,\Bigm|\, \theta_x = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{i^{\ast} \in N} \bigl(\theta(z_{i^{\ast}}')\bigr)_x \text{ for all } \ell\text{-body parameters } x \in \Omega \right\}.
$$

The *backward projection* is then the inverse of forward projection, defined by projecting $w^*$ onto this local manifold:

$$
\operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{B}}^{-1}(w^*) \;\coloneqq\; \operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{D}}(w^*) \;=\; \arg\min_{q \in \mathcal{D}}\, D_{\mathrm{KL}}(w^*,\, q), \qquad \mathcal{D} = \mathcal{M}_\ell^{\ast}(N).
$$

The decoded sample is $z^* = \varphi^{-1}\bigl(\operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{D}}(w^*)\bigr)$. Since $\mathcal{D}$ is $e$-flat (linear constraints on $\theta$), this is again an $m$-projection: a convex KL minimization with a unique solution. The duality between $\mathcal{M}_\ell$ and $\mathcal{M}_\ell^*$ is what makes the round trip clean: $\mathcal{B}$ *zeros out* the high-order interactions and lets the low-order $\theta$-coordinates absorb the data; $\mathcal{D}$ then *clamps* the low-order $\theta$-coordinates to the local-neighborhood mean and lets the high-order ones come from the augmented latent $w^*$. The two sub-manifolds carry complementary information, and the round trip is well-defined because the projection onto either flat sub-manifold is unique.

### The full augmentation loop

The complete algorithm has three steps:

1. **Encode.** Compute $w_i \coloneqq \mathsf{Enc}(z_i) = \operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{B}}(\varphi(z_i))$ for every training point.
2. **Augment.** Generate a new latent $w^* \in \mathcal{B}$ — by perturbation, linear interpolation, or sampling from a kernel density model fit to $\{w_i\}_{i=1}^n$.
3. **Decode.** Compute $z^* \coloneqq \mathsf{Dec}(w^*) = \varphi^{-1}\bigl(\operatorname{Proj}_{\mathcal{M}_\ell^{\ast}(N)}(w^*)\bigr)$ via backward projection on the $k$ nearest neighbors of $w^*$.

  <figcaption><strong>Pseudo-Nonlinear augmentation in one picture.</strong> Each data point $z_i$ embeds via $\varphi$ into the statistical manifold $\mathcal{S}$, then projects forward (dashed) onto the designed base sub-manifold $\mathcal{B}$ as a latent $w_i$. Operations 1 and 3 are *linear* in the intrinsic $\theta$-coordinates of $\mathcal{S}$ but, because $\varphi$ is a normalization and $\theta \mapsto p$ is exponential, they become *curved* transformations in ambient data space — hence **pseudo-nonlinear**. There is no learned decoder; every component is grounded in the actual data geometry.

## Results

### Controllability via $\ell$: visual augmentation on MNIST and CIFAR-10

The full augmentation loop on real images directly visualizes the controllability story. ### Classification accuracy across six datasets

Each classifier is trained on the original set (**OG**) plus a 20% augmentation generated by one method, then evaluated on 20 bootstrapped 50% test subsets. Backbones: ResNet-18 (CIFAR-10), M5 (Speech Commands), MLP (others). Baselines: **STD** (modality-specific transforms), **AE** (autoencoder), **MU** (mixup), **MMU** (manifold mixup).

### Stability on small datasets

The cleanest signal is Connectionist Bench: $n = 208$ samples, $60$ features. Every method's mean improves over **OG** *except* the autoencoder (which collapses under the small-data regime) and **STD** (which has no useful invariance to encode here). PNL is the clear winner on both axes:

| Method | Mean accuracy | Std dev | Δ std vs. OG |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **OG** | 88.10% | 8.58% | — |
| OG + AE | 82.86% | 7.59% | $-0.99$ |

The reduction $8.58 \to 4.54$ is the largest among all baselines, and PNL is the only method that simultaneously raises the mean by more than $5$ points. Beyond Connectionist Bench, PNL also achieves the lowest std dev among augmentation methods on MNIST ($0.21$) and Taiwanese Bankruptcy ($0.47$) — three of the six datasets total — and stays competitive on the rest, lending some general support to stability as a property of energy-based encode–decode rather than a Connectionist Bench artifact.

### Controllability via $\ell$

The $\ell$-body parameter is a true design knob, not a hyperparameter to be tuned. For MNIST, choosing $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{M}_1$ on the reshaped tensor $\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}^{7 \times 2 \times 2 \times 7 \times 2 \times 2}$ retains $\dim(\mathcal{B}) = 17$ — enough to preserve *digit shape* while throwing away pixel-level texture. For CIFAR-10, setting $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{M}_5$, $\mathcal{D} = \mathcal{M}_4^*$ on a higher-order reshape gives $\dim(\mathcal{B}) = 1410$ — large enough to preserve fine *shape × color* coupling (an ostrich keeps its beak and eye colors; the background loses its uncoupled hue). Sweeping $\ell$ traces a clean trade-off: higher $\ell$ → finer reconstruction → but reduced augmentation diversity. No comparable knob exists in mixup, manifold mixup, or autoencoder-based augmentation.

The neighbor count $k$ behaves predictably as well: $k=1$ overfits to a single neighbor (the local manifold $\mathcal{D}$ degenerates), while $k \in \{4, 8, 16\}$ all produce robust, non-trivial augmentations — exactly the qualitative behavior the geometry predicts.
